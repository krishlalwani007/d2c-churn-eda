Business Memo
Objective

Understand customer behavior and identify churn risk before launching retention campaigns.

Key Findings
Customers with higher recency_days show significantly higher churn risk. Customers inactive for longer periods are less likely to return.
Lower engagement customers (low sessions_30d, product_views_30d, and cart_adds_30d) churn more frequently.
Customers with higher return_rate_180d and lower avg_rating_180d are more likely to churn, indicating dissatisfaction.
Support behavior is an important signal. Customers with high ticket_count_90d and high negative_ticket_rate_90d exhibit elevated churn.
Campaign engagement helps retention. Customers with higher campaign_clicks_30d and email_opens_30d show lower churn rates.
Recommendations
Prioritize inactive customers with high recency.
Improve customer support resolution time.
Reduce product returns through quality improvements.
Launch personalized campaigns for low-engagement customers.
Track dissatisfied customers proactively before churn occurs.
